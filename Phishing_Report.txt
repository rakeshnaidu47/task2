Task 2 - Phishing Email Analysis

Email Subject:
Update Notification

Sender:
People's Bank (service@peoples.com)

Objective:
To identify phishing characteristics in a suspicious email sample.

Phishing Indicators Found:

1. Generic Greeting
The email begins with "Dear People's Bank Customer" instead of using the recipient's name. Legitimate organizations usually address customers by name.

2. Urgent Language
The email warns that the account may be temporarily suspended if the user does not act quickly. This creates fear and pressure.

3. Request for Sensitive Information
The email asks the user to confirm banking details through a link. Banks generally do not request sensitive information through emails.

4. Suspicious Link
The email asks the user to click a verification link. Although the link has been removed in this sample, phishing emails commonly use fake websites to steal login credentials.

5. Brand Impersonation
The email pretends to be from People's Bank to gain the recipient's trust.

6. Social Engineering
The message uses fear of identity theft and account suspension to manipulate the user into taking immediate action.

Conclusion:

This email contains multiple phishing indicators such as a generic greeting, urgent language, request for sensitive information, suspicious links, and brand impersonation. Therefore, it should be treated as a phishing email and users should verify such messages through official channels before taking any action.
